1. PRODUCT PRICING & INVENTORY LOGIC
1.1 Pricing Strategy
Base Price Model: All products have a base price per 100g. Display prices are calculated based on selected weight variants.

Weight Variants: Standard weights are 250g, 500g, 1kg, and 2kg. Gift boxes have fixed weights (e.g., "Assortment Box: 1.5kg").

Price Calculation Formula: Display Price = Base Price × (Selected Weight in grams / 100)

Compare Pricing: Optional "original price" display for promotional items. Discount percentage is automatically calculated: Discount % = ((Compare Price - Display Price) / Compare Price) × 100

Currency: All prices in Tunisian Dinar (TND). Display format: "XX.XXX DT" (three decimal places standard for millimes).

1.2 Gift Boxes (Coffrets Cadeaux) Pricing
Fixed Composition Boxes: Pre-defined assortments with fixed content and price (e.g., "Eid Luxury Box" contains specific items).

Custom Boxes (Future): Customers select individual items to build custom gift boxes. Price is sum of selected items plus packaging fee.

Seasonal Pricing: Special gift boxes for holidays (Ramadan, Eid) may have limited-time pricing.

1.3 Inventory Management
Stock Tracking: Real-time inventory for each product variant. System prevents ordering out-of-stock items.

Low Stock Alerts: When stock reaches threshold (configurable per product), admin receives notification.

Backorder Handling: Option to allow pre-orders for out-of-stock popular items with estimated restock date.

Perishable Goods Logic: Products approaching shelf life expiration flagged for priority sale or removal.

2. DELIVERY & FULFILLMENT LOGIC
2.1 Delivery Zones & Pricing
Zone Definition: Geographic areas with standardized delivery fees and timelines.

Zone 1 (Kairouan City): 3 DT fee, same-day delivery for orders before 2 PM.

Zone 2 (Greater Kairouan): 5 DT fee, next-day delivery.

Zone 3 (Tunis City): 7 DT fee, 1-2 day delivery.

Zone 4 (Other Cities): 10-15 DT fee based on distance, 2-3 day delivery.

Free Delivery Threshold: Orders over 50 DT qualify for free delivery in Zones 1-2; 80 DT for Zones 3-4.

2.2 Pickup Options
Store Pickup (Kairouan): Free, available 2 hours after order confirmation during store hours (24/7).

Store Pickup (Tunis): Free, available next day during business hours.

Pickup Time Selection: Customers select pickup date/time slot during checkout.

2.3 Delivery Scheduling
Time Slots: 3-hour windows (9-12, 12-15, 15-18, 18-21).

Cut-off Times: Orders for same-day delivery must be placed before 2 PM.

Blackout Dates: No delivery on national holidays (configurable in admin).

Delivery Instructions: Customers can provide gate codes, building information, or special instructions.

2.4 Delivery Personnel Workflow
Order Batching: System groups deliveries by zone and time slot for efficient routing.

Delivery Agent App (Future): Mobile interface for delivery personnel to view assignments, update statuses, and collect proof of delivery.

3. ORDER PROCESSING & WORKFLOW
3.1 Order Status Lifecycle
text
PENDING → CONFIRMED → PREPARING → READY → IN_TRANSIT → DELIVERED
       ↘ CANCELLED (before PREPARING)
       ↘ REFUNDED (after issues)
PENDING: Order placed, payment pending (for future payment methods).

CONFIRMED: Payment verified, order accepted by store.

PREPARING: Kitchen preparing order (average 1-2 hours for standard orders).

READY: Order packaged and ready for pickup/delivery.

IN_TRANSIT: With delivery personnel en route.

DELIVERED: Successfully delivered to customer.

CANCELLED: Customer or store cancels before preparation.

FAILED: Delivery attempted but failed (customer not available, wrong address).

3.2 Automatic Account Creation Logic
Trigger: Guest checkout completion with valid email.

Account Creation: System creates user with:

Email from order

Name from order

Phone from order

Status: UNCLAIMED

No password set

Account Claim Process:

System sends email with secure claim link (valid 7 days)

User clicks link, sets password

Account status changes to ACTIVE

All guest orders linked to this account

Duplicate Prevention: If email exists, order attaches to existing account (no new account created).

3.3 Order Modification Rules
Additions: Not allowed after order CONFIRMED.

Cancellations: Allowed until status reaches PREPARING.

Time Changes: Delivery/pickup time changes allowed up to 2 hours before scheduled time.

Address Changes: Allowed until order status reaches PREPARING for delivery orders.

4. VALIDATION RULES
4.1 Customer Data Validation
Email: Valid format, not disposable email domains.

Phone: Tunisian format (+216 XX XXX XXX) or international format.

Name: Minimum 2 characters, no special characters except hyphens and spaces.

Address: Required for delivery, validated against known cities/postal codes.

4.2 Order Validation
Minimum Order Amount: 10 DT for delivery, no minimum for pickup.

Maximum Order Amount: 500 DT (for fraud prevention, adjustable).

Weight Limits: Maximum 5kg per delivery (safety limit for delivery personnel).

Time Validation: Orders cannot be scheduled outside business hours.

4.3 Product Configuration Validation
Required Fields: Name (AR/FR), base price, category, at least one image.

Price Validation: Base price must be positive, compare price must be higher than base price if set.

Weight Validation: Variant weights must be positive numbers with valid units (g or kg).

5. PAYMENT PROCESSING LOGIC
5.1 Current Implementation (Phase 1)
Cash on Delivery (COD): Primary method, available for all delivery zones.

Store Pickup Payment: Pay at store upon pickup.

No Online Payments: Initial phase focuses on trust-building with COD.

5.2 Future Payment Methods (Phase 2+)
Bank Transfer: Customers upload transfer receipt, manual verification by admin.

Credit/Debit Cards: Integration with Tunisian payment gateway (CMI, etc.).

Mobile Money: Flouci, D17, or other Tunisian mobile payment solutions.

5.3 Payment Status Flow
text
PENDING → VERIFYING → COMPLETED
       ↘ FAILED → RETRY/REFUND
PENDING: Order placed, payment not yet received.

VERIFYING: Payment received, being verified (for bank transfers).

COMPLETED: Payment confirmed.

FAILED: Payment issue (insufficient funds, fraud flag).

6. CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE RULES
6.1 Multilingual Content Rules
Primary Language: Arabic (right-to-left layout).

Secondary Languages: French, English (left-to-right).

Content Priority:

Arabic content always required

French strongly recommended

English optional

Fallback Logic: If French/English content missing, display Arabic content with language indicator.

6.2 Display Rules
Price Display: Always shows in selected language's format with currency symbol.

Weight Display: Shows metric units with language-appropriate labels ("جرام" / "grammes" / "grams").

Date/Time Format: Respects language and regional preferences (Islamic calendar option for Arabic).

6.3 Customer Communication Rules
Order Confirmation: Sent via email immediately after order placement (all languages available).

Status Updates: Email notifications at key status changes (CONFIRMED, PREPARING, IN_TRANSIT).

Delivery Notification: SMS/WhatsApp notification 30 minutes before delivery (if phone provided).

Marketing Communications: Only sent to customers who opt-in for newsletter.

7. BUSINESS RULES FOR ADMIN
7.1 Order Management Rules
Preparation Time Calculation: System suggests preparation time based on:

Order size (number of items)

Current kitchen load (number of PREPARING orders)

Time of day (peak vs. off-peak)

Priority Orders: Marked for expedited processing (additional fee applicable).

Bulk Order Handling: Orders over 2kg or 10 items flagged for extended preparation time.

7.2 Inventory Reconciliation
Daily Reconciliation: Suggested each morning between system inventory and physical stock.

Waste Tracking: Option to mark inventory reduction due to spoilage/damage.

Production Batches: Track batches for quality control and expiration tracking.

7.3 Customer Management Rules
Loyalty Tracking: Future system for repeat customers (discounts after 5 orders, etc.).

Special Instructions Database: Store customer preferences (e.g., "less sweet", "extra honey").

Blacklist Management: Option to block problematic customers (fraud, non-payment, harassment).

Historical sales patterns

Upcoming holidays/events

Seasonality (Ramadan, Eid, wedding seasons)

Pricing Optimization: Suggest price adjustments based on:

Competitor pricing (manual input)

Cost changes

Demand elasticity

Promotion Effectiveness: Track impact of discounts/offers on sales volume and revenue.

9.1 Holiday & Peak Period Rules

Minimum order amounts may apply

Advance ordering encouraged (7-day calendar)

Special holiday packaging/gift options

9.3 Quality Issues & Complaints

Quality issues: Full refund or replacement

Late delivery: Partial refund (percentage based on delay)

Wrong items: Replacement plus discount on next order

Complaint Resolution Workflow: 24-hour response time target, escalation path for unresolved issues.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

Audit Trail: All business rule applications should be logged for troubleshooting and compliance.

Exception Logging: When business rules prevent an action (e.g., order outside delivery hours), the specific rule violation should be clearly communicated to the user.

Regional Compliance: All rules should consider Tunisian business regulations, consumer protection laws, and cultural norms.

Seasonal Adjustments: Business rules should account for seasonal variations in demand, supply, and operational capacity.
