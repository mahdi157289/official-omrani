2. Executive Summary
The Makroudh Omrani E-commerce Platform is a modern, full-stack website designed to transform a traditional Tunisian pastry shop's digital presence. Currently reliant on Facebook and phone orders, the business seeks to streamline sales, enhance customer experience, and build a scalable brand online.

The platform serves local customers in Tunisia and the global diaspora, offering an intuitive way to browse and order authentic Makroudh. It features a powerful admin panel, giving the owners direct control over products, content, and analytics. Success means increased order volume, reduced operational friction, and establishing a premium digital storefront for a beloved traditional product.

3. Problem Statement
Problem: Makroudh Omrani's sales are constrained by a manual, fragmented process (Facebook messages, phone calls, WhatsApp). This leads to order errors, difficulty managing inventory/promotions, no customer account history, and an inability to serve non-local or international customers effectively.

Who Experiences This: The store owners (order management headaches), customers (inconvenient ordering, lack of transparency), and potential customers outside immediate reach.

Current Workaround: Using Facebook as a catalog and coordinating delivery via phone calls and messaging apps.

Cost of Not Solving: Lost sales due to friction, inability to scale, stagnant growth, and vulnerability to competitors with modern online stores. Manual processes limit time for business development.

4. Goals and Objectives
4.1 Business Goals
Increase online sales by 40% within 6 months of launch.

Reduce order management time for owners by 50%.

Expand customer base to serve the Tunisian diaspora within 12 months.

Establish Makroudh Omrani as a leading digital brand for traditional Tunisian sweets.

4.2 Product Goals
Provide a seamless, 3-minute checkout from product discovery to order confirmation.

Automatically create a customer profile upon first order to enable repeat business.

Give administrators full real-time control over all site content and products.

Deliver site performance exceeding a 90 Lighthouse score.

4.3 Success Criteria (Measurable)
Conversion: >15% of unique visitors to the shop page complete a purchase.

Usability: Admin can add a new product with images via the CMS in under 2 minutes.

Performance: Site achieves Core Web Vitals targets and loads in <2.5s on 3G.

Adoption: 80% of orders shift from phone/Facebook to the website within 3 months.

5. Target Audience
5.1 Primary Users
Local Tunisian Customers: Residents of Kairouan, Tunis, and other cities. Age 25-65. Comfortable with smartphones and online shopping (like via Facebook Marketplace). Seeks convenience and reliable delivery. Primary language: Arabic.

Tunisian Diaspora: Living in Europe, North America, etc. Age 30-70. Tech-savvy. Seeks authentic taste from home to ship to themselves or as gifts to family in Tunisia. Languages: Arabic & French/English.

5.2 Secondary Users
Store Admin (Owner): Needs full oversight, analytics, and control. Not necessarily highly technical.

Store Editor (Employee): Needs to update product status, view orders, and manage basic content.

5.3 User Personas
Persona 1: Amira, The Local Customer

Role: Working professional in Tunis.

Goal: Quickly order fresh Makroudh for a family gathering with clear delivery timing.

Pain Point: Hesitant to call, forgets prices, wants to see what she's ordering.

Tech Skill: Intermediate.

Usage: Uses her smartphone to browse, select a 1kg box, and schedule evening delivery.

Persona 2: Sami, The Diaspora Customer

Role: Tunisian living in France.

Goal: Send a traditional gift box to his parents in Sfax for Eid.

Pain Point: Cannot find trustworthy online stores that ship authentic products within Tunisia.

Tech Skill: Advanced.

Usage: Uses desktop to read the "Our Story" page, chooses a luxury gift box, and enters his parents' Tunisian address for delivery.

Persona 3: Mohamed, The Store Owner

Role: Owner of Makroudh Omrani.

Goal: Run his business efficiently, understand what's selling, and promote special events.

Pain Point: Drowning in WhatsApp messages, can't track sales trends easily.

Tech Skill: Basic.

Usage: Logs into the admin dashboard daily to confirm new orders, check the analytics page for weekly bestsellers, and create a new "Ramadan Special" product collection.
